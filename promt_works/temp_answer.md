Based on information available up to May 2025, Iran's economy has been **contracting or experiencing very weak growth**, struggling under persistent challenges. Here are the main contributing factors:

1. **Sustained International Sanctions:** Comprehensive U.S. sanctions severely restrict Iran's oil exports, access to the global financial system, and foreign investment, crippling its main revenue source and ability to conduct international trade.

2. **Structural and Domestic Policy Issues:** Chronic problems include a large, inefficient public sector, costly subsidies, a multi-tiered exchange rate system that causes distortions, and years of underinvestment in critical infrastructure and domestic industry.

3. **Regional Instability and Geopolitical Isolation:** Conflicts and tensions in the region deter investment, increase security costs, and divert resources. Iran's geopolitical stance further limits its economic partnerships and trade routes.

4. **High Inflation and Currency Depreciation:** Years of monetizing fiscal deficits and a lack of foreign currency have led to hyperinflation, eroding purchasing power, increasing poverty, and causing significant business uncertainty.

5. **Limited Economic Diversification:** Despite efforts, the economy remains overly reliant on hydrocarbons. Non-oil sectors are underdeveloped and insufficient to offset sanctions' impact or provide broad-based employment, leaving the economy vulnerable to external shocks.

**Important Note:** This assessment is based on trends and data leading up to mid-2025. The situation may have evolved since then, and current conditions could differ. For the most recent picture, checking up-to-date sources from international financial institutions or economic analysts is recommended.
